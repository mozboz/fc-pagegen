Molyvos: Vital arrival info

Back to top

Welcome to the Greek island of Lesvos. From wherever you land on the island, you will need to travel to the capital, Mytilene. In Mytilene you will be registered as arriving in Greece. You need to be registered to continue your journey on a ferry or plane, take taxis or stay in hotels. You cannot move around Greece legally without completing the registration process.

Near Mytilene there are registration centres where the police will record personal information from you and your family members or those traveling with you. You may be asked for fingerprints as well. This does not prevent your onward journey. Please cooperate with the police if you are requested to provide fingerprints.Once your registration is completed, you will receive a registration document from the police with your information.

This police note is valid for 6 months, but is only valid in Greece. It is recommended that you make a copy of the document in case you lose or damage it. There are photocopiers available for use at the registration centres.

Travel from Molyvos to Mytilene:

  • The registration centres are between 65-70km from Molyvos in a city called Mytilene. It is a 1.5 hour bus journey or approximately 16 – 20 hour walk.
  • Local organisations, NGOs such as the IRC, MSF, Save the Children, Mercy Corps and the UN are operating free bus services to Mytilene, stopping at the registration centres. Once arriving in Molyvos, you will be directed to a location where you can find the bus service.
  • Please be aware that there could be a significant wait for the bus, depending on the number of arrivals and the number of buses. Depending on when you arrive, this may include staying the night at the bus station. We kindly ask for your patience.
  • You are permitted to walk to Mytilene. For a reasonably fit person, this trip could take about 2 days. Please be advised that the walk is along a highway with sharp turns and many hills. If you attempt to walk, do not walk in the middle of the road, and watch for cars.
  • Walking is strenuous, and is not recommended, particularly for families with small children or people with mobility or health concerns.
  • If you choose to walk, along the road for the first 23km there are water taps where you can access free water. Tap water in Greece is safe to drink. You will not get sick.
  • After 23km you will arrive at a location called Kalloni. In Kalloni there is a site where you can sleep for the night or take a rest before you continue the rest of the way.
  • You cannot take a taxi. Without registering, according to the government you have illegally entered Greece. Taxis cannot take you. It is also illegal for private cars to drive you.
  • Please be advised that if you do take a taxi or a private car you are putting yourself and the driver at risk of arrest.

Tickets for a ferry can be purchased at the Port through travel agencies or from the ticket booth in Kara Tepe. Tickets can also be purchased online with a credit card.

    • Required: Police note

  • Blue Star I of Blue Star Ferries:

    • Prices: economy seating 45.50 € or numbered seating 48.50 € or 73 € for a two person cabin or 72 € for a four person cabin or 55.50 € for a family pack
    • Trip duration is 12 hours to Athens

    • Prices: economy seatings costs 60 €. The ticket for a four person cabin is 70 € each and for a two person cabin, is 72 € each.

    • Prices: Adult tickets 60 € with lunch. Cabin tickets are an additional 10 -15 €. Cabins can not be purchased through travel agencies but only directly from the ferry company.

Registration

Back to top

How to register

  • Nationals from Syria, Palestine, Sudan, Somalia and Eritrea can be registered in Kara Tepe. Any personal identification documents available need to be presented at the point of registration. The registration process includes review of identification documentation, questions, fingerprinting and photo. Upon completion, a police note is provided which allows those individuals listed to stay in Greece for maximum of 6 months before requiring renewal. Each adult registers separately and any children below the age of 18 are registered on their parent’s police note.
  • Nationalities not listed above, must be registered in Moria, with or without a passport, and successful registration results in receipt of a police note that allows them to stay in Greece for a maximum of one month. This registration can be renewed at one month.
  • All nationalities must be registered prior to applying for asylum in Greece.
  • In Moria there is an office for asylum requests that is open Monday through Friday 9:00 am-3:00 pm. Processing of asylum requests can take approximately 1.5 - 3 months. If asylum is granted, refugees will receive travel documents which permits travel abroad or residence in Greece for up to 3 years. For more information please contact: 22510 32323 (Languages available English-Greek)

Money Transfers

Western Union

Western Union (Tel: 2251031777) is located in Mitonis Travel Agency. You can receive maximum of 6000€. To receive money at this location, you are required to present your passport or national identification, the name of the person sending the money, the address and country of the sender. Western Union is open Monday through Saturday 9:00am-8:00pm.

Post Office

The Post Office also has a Western Union (Tel: 22510 28823). To access money here, you will need your passport or police note with photo. Their office is open Monday to Friday, 7:00am - 2:00pm. At this branch you can receive maximum of 1800€ per month without a transaction fee.

Money Exchange

National Bank of Greece

29 Kountourioti P. Street. (Tel: 22510 34154). Police note or passport is required. They exchange American Dollars and Turkish Liras. The bank will change up to $300 with a 6€ bank fee. If you need to change a higher amount, the bank fee increases to10€.

Peiraios Bank

17 Kountourioti Street.(Tel. 22510 34100) To change money you will need yourpolice note and passport, as well as a mobile phone number in Greece. They exchange American Dollars and Turkish Liras for a 6€ transaction fee.

Bank of Greece

9 Kountourioti Street. (Tel. 22510 28474) To exchange money you will need your police note or a passport. They exchange American dollars free of charge.
